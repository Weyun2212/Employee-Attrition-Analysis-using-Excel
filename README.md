# Employee-Attrition-Analysis-using-Excel
This project focuses on analyzing employee attrition data within an organization using Excel. The objective is to understand the factors driving employee turnover, identify trends in attrition, and uncover insights that HR professionals and management can use to improve retention strategies.

üìä Project Overview

Employee attrition, or turnover, is a major challenge for organizations as it increases hiring costs, reduces productivity, and impacts team morale.
This project analyzes a structured attrition dataset to answer questions such as:

- Which departments and job roles have the highest attrition?

- How do age, gender, education, and marital status affect turnover?

- Does job satisfaction or work environment satisfaction influence attrition?

- Are salary hikes helping to retain employees?

- Which groups of employees are most at risk of leaving?

This Excel project provides a clear and comprehensive view of these factors through dashboards and visualizations.

1. Job & Organizational Factors

2. Work Experience & Satisfaction

- Low job satisfaction and low environment satisfaction strongly correlate with attrition.

- Employees working frequent overtime are significantly more likely to leave.

- Both average and high performers show attrition, indicating possible lack of recognition or growth.

3. Demographic Factors

- Employees aged 25‚Äì34 have the highest attrition rate.

- Single employees leave more frequently than married employees.

- Shorter commute distances do not reduce attrition, indicating workplace rather than commuting issues.

- Attrition is higher among male employees in this dataset.

4. Compensation

- Employees in the low income band show the highest attrition.

- Salary hike percentage does not significantly influence retention.
